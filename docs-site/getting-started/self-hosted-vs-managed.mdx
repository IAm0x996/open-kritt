title: "Self-hosted vs managed"
description: "Run open·kritt yourself, or have Kritt scan your repo with proven workflows."
---

open·kritt comes in two forms. The **open-source project** is the full engine you run on
your own infrastructure. The **managed scan** is a service where the Kritt team runs its
own proven, private workflows against a target you provide.

## Self-hosted (open source)

The project in this repository is licensed under **AGPL-3.0** and is fully self-hosted.
You get the entire platform - the workflow builder, scans, post-scripts, severity
rankers, agent skills, and the orchestration engine - running on your own machines with
your own model provider keys.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="You own everything" icon="server">
    You operate the application, database, prompts, and credentials on your infrastructure.
    Scan content is sent to the model endpoint you choose.
  </Card>
  <Card title="You author the workflows" icon="pen-ruler">
    The engine is yours, but the vulnerability-hunting strategy is up to you. You build
    and tune the workflows that decide what "good" looks like.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Warning>
  Self-hosted does not mean offline. The engine sends repository contents and prompts to
  the configured Codex/OpenAI, Anthropic, or OpenRouter endpoint. Tool-enabled scan jobs
  also have direct internet access so agents can fetch tools, dependencies, and public
  research. Choose providers whose data handling matches your code's sensitivity, run the
  stack on a dedicated Docker host or VM, and review the
  [threat model](https://github.com/Kritt-ai/open-kritt/blob/main/docs/threat-model.md).
</Warning>

<Note>
  AGPL-3.0 is a strong network-copyleft license: if you modify open·kritt and run it as a
  network service, you must make your modified source available to its users. See the
  [LICENSE](https://github.com/Kritt-ai/open-kritt/blob/main/LICENSE) for the full terms.
</Note>

## Managed scan (Kritt runs it for you)

The hard-won part of security research is the workflow itself - the decomposition,
prompts, and guardrails that reliably surface real bugs. Those proven workflows are
Kritt's core IP, so a subset of them is kept private.

With a managed scan you don't rebuild that expertise. You point us at a repository and we
run our proven workflows against it, then hand you the ranked, verified findings.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Submit your target">
    Give us the repository you want scanned.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Complete KYC and vetting">
    Because running powerful vulnerability-finding workflows against a codebase can be
    abused by malicious actors, we require identity verification (KYC) and a rigorous
    review to confirm there is no malicious intent.
  </Step>
  <Step title="We scan and deliver">
    Once you're approved, we run our private workflows and return the findings.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  The managed scan exists precisely because this capability is dual-use. KYC and manual
  approval are mandatory - we do not run scans for unverified requesters.
</Warning>

## Which should I use?

| | Self-hosted | Managed scan |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **License / access** | AGPL-3.0, open source | Kritt-operated service |
| **Runs on** | Your infrastructure | Kritt infrastructure |
| **Workflows** | You build your own | Kritt's proven private workflows |
| **Model access and data egress** | Your keys/login; content goes to your selected endpoint | Handled by Kritt |
| **Onboarding** | Clone and run | KYC + vetting required |
| **Best for** | Teams that want full control and their own strategy | Teams that want proven results without building the workflows |
